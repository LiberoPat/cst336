What is Scrum

Scrum is an agile way to manage a software project. Rather than a succinct methodology, scrum is rather a way to view and manage a process.
There is not a team leader in scrum who dictates everyone's role, rather, every team member works cross functionally. Another feature of scrum is sprint,
which is the scrum synonym of no more than a month taking an idea, to a programmed function to be tested, eventually sent in to be a feature or added to a
system.

There are short daily meetings where each member of the scrum team discusses what they
have worked on, what they will work on today and then the next day.

At the end of a sprint, the team demonstrates some new functionality that they come up
with to stakeholders, and then the team reflects on improving for next time.

Scrum Roles

  1. Scrum Master: Doesn't provide direction, but improves every team members focus to stay on task.
  2. Product Owner: Writes down the requirements for each sprint and has the understanding the direction the team will take
  3. The Team: Each tember will perform their task unique to them, however in scrum, it is expected to work beyond their comfort zone.
